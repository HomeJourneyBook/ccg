Rules

The Idea

Two players. One device. Tea defends the Tavern, Jeet defends the Core — each with 20 HP. You build a field of Travelers and Legendaries, cast spells, activate artifacts and worlds. Bring your opponent's base to zero first.

What's On a Card

Every card shows: its cost (top left — Essence to play it), its art, its name, and for creatures — HP (heart) and ATK (sword) at the bottom. Below that is its ability text.

Example: a Szarg costs 1 Essence, has 1 HP and 2 ATK, and reads "Vanguard" — it can act the turn it arrives.

Essence

Essence is your resource. You start with a maximum of 1 and gain +1 each turn — so by turn 5 you have 5 to spend. It fully refills at the start of every turn.

Once per turn you may burn a card from your hand: it goes to the Void forever, but your Essence maximum permanently increases by 1.

A Turn, Step by Step

1. Your Essence refills. Worlds, Artifacts, and field effects trigger automatically.

2. Act freely — play cards, attack, use abilities — in any order.

3. Press End Turn (or Space) when done. Draw 1 card, then your opponent takes over.

Creatures

Travelers and Legendaries are creatures. When played, they enter sleeping (dashed border) and cannot act until your next turn. Tap to select, then tap the enemy to attack. The defender hits back simultaneously — unless Feared or Invisible.

Spells

Spells take effect immediately when played, then go to the Void.

Worlds

A World sits below your battlefield and passively affects the game. Only one active at a time — playing a second replaces the first. Activates at the start of each turn.

Artifacts

Up to two Artifacts can be active at once. Some trigger automatically each turn; others are active — tap to use once per turn. Artifacts sleep their first turn.

Graveyard vs Void

The Graveyard (☠) holds creatures killed in battle — they can be revived. The Void holds burned cards, spells, and replaced Worlds — nothing retrieves them.

Glossary

Vanguard

Can act on the same turn it enters the field.

Sleeping

Entered the field this turn, cannot act yet. Wakes at the start of your next turn. Vanguard skips this state.

Provoke

All enemy attacks must target this creature.

Bushido

ALL attacks — including Pierce — must target this creature. The strongest form of forced targeting.

Pierce

Ignores Provoke. Can attack any enemy or the base directly. Does not bypass Bushido.

Fear

When this creature attacks, the target skips its next turn and deals no counter-attack. A Feared creature cannot attack but Provoke still applies.

Invisible

Cannot be targeted while allies are on the field. Deals no counter-attack when attacked.

Rage

Gains +1 ATK permanently each time it attacks. Resets on death.

Burn

Target catches fire when attacked. Burning creatures lose 1 HP at turn start. Permanent until removed or death.

Regen

Restores X HP to itself at the start of each turn, up to its maximum.

Aura

Passive bonus for all allies while this card is on the field. Lost immediately when the source leaves. Multiple auras stack.

Max HP

If at full health when maxHP increases, current HP rises too (3/3 → 4/4). If wounded, only max grows (2/3 → 2/4).

Squad

When 3+ Travelers of the same Gate type are on your field simultaneously, all receive a squad bonus. Lost the moment the squad drops below 3.

Revive

Bring a creature back from the Graveyard at full HP. Enters sleeping. On Enter effects do not re-trigger.

Raise

Revival at 1 HP instead of full. Used automatically by Phlegmor each turn.

Lore

Before This

The Archive exists. Somewhere in the Valley, Teantist still brews Chaetron and translates the Book — page by page, year by year. Shen still tends the Tavern at the crossroads of all Worlds. Travelers still walk the Path, collecting what remains of vanished civilizations.

That is the record. What follows is what the record did not anticipate.

The Fracture

No one knows exactly when Jeet stopped being a slow erosion and became something else. The change was not announced. It was noticed in gaps — a World that Shen stopped receiving reports from. A Traveler who returned speaking in patterns that did not belong to them. A section of the Archive that Teantist quietly sealed.

By the time the Tavern held a council, Jeet had already claimed a third of the known Worlds. Not destroyed them. Claimed them. The Travelers inside did not die. They remained — but they walked for Jeet now.

Two Sides

The line did not form cleanly. Some Travelers chose. Others were taken. A few — Ryvlen, the Reaper — had walked so close to Jeet for so long that no one was entirely surprised when they stopped returning to the Tavern.

What remained of the Path split into two currents. Those who still walked toward the Great Return, toward the Essence, toward Tea. And those who now walked deeper into dissolution — whether by choice, corruption, or something in between that the Archive had no word for.

What Is at Stake

The Worlds between them are the contested ground. Each one holds memories — fragments of what civilizations once knew, preserved in the imprints that Unique Travelers are built to carry. If Jeet claims enough of them, the Great Return becomes impossible. Not because the Essence disappears. Because there will be nothing left that remembers what it was.

Teantist has not sealed the Archive. He continues to send Schemes. Whatever he found in those pages he will not translate — he has apparently decided it does not change what must be done next.

Now

Two forces move through the remaining Worlds. One carries tea, memory, and the long patience of those who still believe the Path leads somewhere worth reaching. The other carries the strange clarity of dissolution — the knowledge that entropy, too, has its own direction.

The outcome of this is not recorded yet.
